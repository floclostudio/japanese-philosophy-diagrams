土間 doma
Earthen-floor workspace. The transitional space between outside and inside, where shoes are removed.
間 as the threshold space — neither fully outside nor inside.
居間 ima
Living room. Literally 'being-space' — where one dwells and exists.
間 defines where life unfolds.
客間 kyakuma
Guest room. A dedicated space for receiving visitors, often more formal than the family living areas.
間 as social architecture — space shaped by relationship.
床の間 tokonoma
Alcove in a Japanese room, displaying a scroll or flower arrangement. The most honored space, where guests are seated facing it.
間 as negative space with presence — empty yet full of meaning.
空間 kūkan
Space. Literally 'emptiness-between' — the void that exists between things.
間 names the interval itself, not the things that bound it.
時間 jikan
Time. Literally 'moment-between' — time understood as intervals, not as continuous flow.
間 reveals time as structured by pauses, not just duration.
合間 aima
A break, an interval, spare time between activities. The gap that opens between one thing and the next.
間 as opportunity — the space where choice becomes possible.
間合い maai
Combative distance. In martial arts, the precise spacing between opponents — close enough to strike, far enough to react.
間 as charged tension — relaxed but ready, 'murderous calm.'
間 ma
Pause, timing, the pregnant moment. In performance arts (rakugo, Noh, comedy), the silence that gives shape to what comes next.
間 in its purest form — the interval that contains everything.
人間 ningen
Human being. In Chinese (人间 rénjiān), this means 'the human world' — a place. In Japanese, it means the person themselves. Japan redefined 'human' as something that exists in the interval.
間 as ontology — to be human is to be constituted by relationship.
世間 seken
Society, the social world. Not 'the worldly realm' but the web of social relations itself — what others think, communal judgment, shared reality.
間 as the space between selves that forms collective reality.
仲間 nakama
Companion, comrade, peer. Not 'someone in your circle' but the relationship-between that makes companionship possible.
間 names the bond, not the individuals it connects.
間抜け manuke
Fool, idiot. Literally 'missing 間' — to lack the interval is to be disconnected from timing, from others, from the fabric of things.
間 by its absence — without it, one is adrift.
